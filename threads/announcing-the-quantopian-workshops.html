Our meetups have been a really great way to present some of the content from the Lecture Series, but we wanted to give attendees the ability to really dive into the material and get their hands dirty.

To that end we are launching a series of in-person full-day workshops in which we will alternate between lecture presentations and hands-on exercises. The goal is to give you an understanding of computational finance building blocks that you can use to further your own knowledge going forward. The curriculum we use has been vetted by professors at universities including Harvard, MIT, Stanford, and Cornell ORIE Manhattan.

We are planning more workshops in the future, including many international locations. Let us know where you’re located and we’ll see if we can set something up.

More workshops are coming soon! Stay tuned.

Thanks,
Delaney

Sally Sanford

@James,

There are many great, free resources for learning python. For example, this Harvey Mudd course hosted by edX, this Lynda course (free if you can finish it in a week) or any of these:

  • Learn Python the Hard Way
  • Python for Beginners
  • Code Academy

I have a more comprehensive list hiding in my folders that, when found, I will use to update this short list.

However, you have a legitimate gripe if you were to compare building out your own python environment and learning the quirks and limitations of the Quantopian sandbox. For me, it was far, far easier to develop my own environment and a whole let less stressful debugging my errors instead of conforming to Quantopian's rather limiting implementations of both data and code. YMMV.

Delaney Mackenzie

Hey Sam,

We appreciate that $675 if not a small amount, and we did think a lot about the correct direction to go here. We want to clarify that what we’re charging for is events in addition to our normal schedule. We are still running the same free meetups and will be presenting the same lecture content as before. The workshops were built in response to the many requests we have gotten from our community for this type of hands-on education, and after noticing the success of this material when presented at top schools. Given how expensive other workshops teaching the same content are, we’re confident that we can offer the best education at a below market rate.

In addition to the hundreds of hours that we put into building these workshops, we use the profits to enable more free events in previously unreachable locations. Because of the workshops, we hope to be able to fund trips to Europe and Asia. We’re also experimenting with ideas for a cheaper online course option, and welcome your continued feedback.

Thanks,
Delaney
