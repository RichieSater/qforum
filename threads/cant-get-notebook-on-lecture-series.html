Mark Olton

Rather than allow 3rd party cookies wholesale, you can just allow the domain that quantopian is talking to. For Chrome you can go to Settings->Advanced->Content Settings->Cookies and add the following into the "Allow" section: "[*.]dynoquant.com". This will whitelist 3rd party cookies only from the dynoquant.com domain.

Jonathan Kamens

Hi Ulf et al,

The cookies that are required for our research environment to work are not third-party tracking cookies, they are specific to our platform and data about them is not shared with anyone outside of Quantopian.

The "dynoquant.com" domain, whose cookies are blocked by some add-ons, is owned and controlled by Quantopian. We run the research notebook infrastructure on this separate domain for security reasons. The cookies which are being blocked are the session cookies required for research notebooks to work.

We know that the user experience when these cookies are blocked is sub-optimal. We have a fix in the works to detect this problem and display a more useful message notifying you that you need to enable third-party cookies. We have also looked into technical solutions to eliminate the need for these cookies, but so far we have been unable to find one which satisfies our security requirements. Unfortunately, sometimes, as in this case, competing security requirements butt heads against each other.

Regarding GDPR, our terms of use and privacy policy mention that we use cookies. Consenting to our terms of use and privacy policy is required in order to create a Quantopian account. Furthermore, we are in the process of preparing revisions to both documents. When we roll out the revisions, all community members will be asked to consent to the new versions before continuing to use the platform.

We are serious about our obligation to protect our members' privacy and adhere to the requirements of the GDPR and other jurisdictions' privacy laws and regulations. If you have any additional concerns, feel free to reach out to us directly or comment here.

Also, if the workarounds mentioned above for fixing this problem don't work for you, please reach out directly to our support team so we can help.

Regards,

Jonathan Kamens
CISO
