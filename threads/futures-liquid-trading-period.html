Jamie McCorriston

Hi Pravin,

Have you tried setting the slippage to 0 to see if that helps? To override the default slippage models on the futures calendar, you need to use this line of code: 

set_slippage(us_futures=slippage.FixedSlippage(0))

One other thing to note with the futures calendar is that the only holidays that are handled normally are those that apply to all futures - Christmas Day, US New Years, and US Independence Day (July 4th). Outside of these holidays, there are other holidays that are observed by a subset of exchanges. Sometimes, there are even holidays that are observed by a subset of futures on the same exchange. For the holidays that are not observed by all futures, they will simply show up as having 0 volume on the given day, so you'll see orders on that day that appear not to fill.

For the partial orders, I'm not as sure. Can you try the zero-slippage model and let me know what happens?

what timezone is the volume data timing in ?

Dan Whitnable

@Adib Zoghbi All the pricing methods on Quantopian return times in UTC. This includes futures data. UTC times can easily be converted to US/Eastern time with the pandas tz_convert method. The volumes returned by the history method in the notebook can be converted to 'market' time something like this

history(future, fields='volume', frequency='minute', start='2016-04-01', end='2016-10-01').tz_convert('US/Eastern')

Adib Zoghbi

@Dan thanks for the prompt answer. This is clear. One more question, I see that the data comes either minutes or daily. Is there a way to change it to other formats. For examples, 4 hours, or hourly?

Dan Whitnable

There isn't a built in method to get different frequencies. However, one can use the pandas resample method (https://pandas.pydata.org/pandas-docs/version/0.18/generated/pandas.DataFrame.resample.html). If one wanted to get hourly volume do something like this

# resample every 1 hour and every 4 hours  
volume_1h = volume.tz_convert('US/Eastern').resample('1h', closed='right', label='right').sum()  
volume_4h = volume.tz_convert('US/Eastern').resample('4h', closed='right', label='right').sum()

One needs to decide how to resample. Volume is easy and should typically be the sum of all the minute volumes. However price is a judgement call. One could use last (ie the close of each sample bin) or potentially mean or median. There is also a bit more discussion on the various resample parameters in this post https://www.quantopian.com/posts/how-to-use-the-resample-correctly
